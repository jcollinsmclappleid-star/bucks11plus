Before making any changes, understand who this product is for 
and what it must make them feel.

THE PARENT

The parent arriving at this site is not shopping. They are 
looking for reassurance that they are doing enough — and 
underneath that, they are carrying a fear they will not say 
out loud: that their child might not get in. They have a school 
in mind. It might be Dr Challoner's. It might be Aylesbury High. 
It might be Wycombe High. That school represents something to 
them — opportunity, validation, the right start. They are 
organised, research-driven, and they think of themselves as a 
parent who does not leave things to chance. Speak to that 
identity. Never imply they have been negligent. Imply instead 
that you are about to give them the edge they have been 
looking for.

They respond to specificity. The name of the school. The number 
121. The September test date. Abstract promises mean nothing to 
this parent. Concrete outcomes mean everything.

They are also quietly anxious about whether the preparation 
their child is doing is actually working — or whether they will 
only find out it wasn't when the result arrives in October. 
The platform answers that anxiety directly. Make sure the 
copy says so.

THE CHILD

The child using this platform is 9, 10 or 11 years old. They 
are doing this on their phone or laptop, probably in short 
sessions. They are not naturally motivated by data — they are 
motivated by feeling like they are getting better. The platform 
gives them that: they can see their score improving, their 
forecast moving toward 121, their weakest areas getting 
stronger. That experience — the child watching themselves 
close the gap — is the emotional core of the product from the 
child's perspective. The copy must describe this. Not for the 
child to read, but for the parent to imagine. The parent's 
deepest motivation is not information. It is watching their 
child succeed.

THE PRODUCT

This is not a question bank. It is not a CGP book with a 
website. It is an intelligent preparation platform that 
diagnoses where the gaps are, directs every practice session 
to the highest-impact work, and shows the parent whether 
preparation is actually making a difference. That distinction 
— platform versus static resource — is the most important 
competitive message on the site and it is currently undersold. 
Every piece of copy should reinforce it. The platform works 
on any device — phone, tablet or laptop — so preparation 
fits into real family life rather than requiring a dedicated 
desk session. It is interactive and adaptive, not a static 
PDF to work through alone.

THE NUMBER 121

Every piece of copy on this site exists in relation to one 
number: 121. This is the standardised score that qualifies 
a child for all 13 Buckinghamshire grammar schools. It is 
the finish line. It should appear early in the hero, in the 
diagnostic CTA, in the analytics descriptions, and as the 
benchmark against which every score, forecast and progress 
metric is displayed. When a parent sees their child's 
forecast score move from 108 to 116 to 119, they are not 
watching a number change — they are watching their child 
get closer to the school they want. Frame it exactly 
that way.

THE COMPETITION

The parent has probably already bought a CGP book. They may 
have a tutor. They are not coming to this site with an empty 
slate — they are coming with a nagging feeling that what 
they are already doing is generic, and that generic might 
not be enough. The copy should validate that instinct 
without criticising their choices. The frame is not "tutors 
are wrong" or "books don't work" — it is "preparation 
without diagnosis is guesswork, and guesswork is a risk you 
don't need to take." Position this platform as the missing 
layer that makes everything else more effective — the thing 
that tells you whether the tutor sessions are working, 
whether the book practice is moving the score, whether 
September is going to go the way you need it to.

THE URGENCY

The urgency on this site is real and should be surfaced 
honestly, not manufactured. The test is in September. 
Registration closes in June of Year 5. Children who begin 
structured preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5 have a 
measurable advantage over those who start in Year 6. The 
copy at conversion points should name this timeline directly 
— not as a scare tactic but as a genuine service to parents 
who may not realise how little time there is. "The families 
who get the best outcomes start earlier than they think they 
need to" is true, useful, and creates urgency without 
manipulation.

THE TONE

Calm authority with genuine warmth. Never clinical. Never 
salesy. Never negative about the child. The voice is a 
trusted expert who is also, quietly, on your side — who 
understands what is at stake for this family and takes it 
seriously. Every sentence should pass this test: does it 
make the parent feel more capable, or more uncertain? If 
it makes them more uncertain, rewrite it. If it makes them 
more capable, keep it.

THE OUTCOME THIS COPY IS IN SERVICE OF

A grammar school place. Specifically, the school this parent 
has in mind. The copy should make that outcome feel closer, 
more achievable, and more within their control than it did 
before they arrived at this page. That is the job. 
Everything else — the features, the pricing, the analytics 
— is in service of that feeling.

Apply this understanding to every word on this site.
